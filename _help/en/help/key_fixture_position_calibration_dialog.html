Tutorial – Fixture Position Calibration Dialog

There are two different modes for this dialog: the Absolute and the Free mode.

The Auto Calibration system is a system for the software to position moving light fixtures based on four points of orientation on the floor. Auto Calibration implies a high precision during orientation of the parameters.

Open the Stage View and then open the options of the Stage View.

How to Calibrate 

  1. Mark the 4 physical points of orientation A,B,C and D on your stage. The 4 points have to form a rectangle.
  2. Enter the physical position of points A to D depending on the mode:
    • Absolute mode: Select all fixtures and enter the coordinates of point A (X,Y,Z) and the correct distances to the points B and D – C is automatically calculated.
    • Free mode: Select all fixtures and enter the correct coordinates of point A (X,Y,Z), B (X,Y,Z), C (X,Y,Z) – D is automatically calculated.
  3. Focus each fixture to position A using pan and tilt attributes.
  4. Select all fixtures again and press Store  A. The position of the current selection is stored in A.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for B, C and D.
  6. Select all fixtures again and tap Do Calibration. The physical positions of the fixture selection are calibrated.

The fixtures are moved and rotated in the Stage View.

Important:
If the fixtures are positioned in an odd manner in the Stage View after the calibration, tap Find Alternative Fixture Position. 

For more information see Calibration Pop-up.

It is possible to calibrate each fixture individually within a separate physical rectangle. An asterisk '(*)' means that the physical positions of A,B,C or D differ in the current selection.
